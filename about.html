Her Story

The Work and the Woman

Adi Marie is an indigenous Mesoamerican and Caribbean curandera, therapeutic mystic, and Earth medicine practitioner whose work is rooted in an eco-poetic philosophy — understanding humanity as a cosmic and local expression of divinity and living consciousness.

Over sixteen years of somatic practice, she discovered what her clients already knew: the body carries everything. The memory of what was done to us. The grief of what was lost. The blueprint of who we are becoming. She learned, through touch, that healing cannot be compartmentalized into the physical or the spiritual — that the flesh and the sacred are one continuous fabric, and that working with one without the other is an incomplete offering.

From this knowing, a new practice emerged — one that weaves somatic bodywork, shamanic guidance, plant medicine facilitation, herbalism, Dagara-lineage divination, and mythopoetic embodiment work into a coherent whole. Not a menu of techniques, but a living ceremony of care.

She studies with elders Ivan Chocron, Yvonne, and Chad Charles, and her practice is guided by ancestral wisdom, ritual storytelling, and deep reverence for the more-than-human world.

True healing is a reunion with the cosmic web of existence — where body, mind, and timeless soul harmonize in the miracle of life itself.
The Place

The Haven

Adi Marie holds her ceremonial-therapeutic practice in Topanga Canyon at The Haven — a studio space set within the canyon's living landscape, where the boundary between the human and the wild is thin and porous.

The Haven is not a clinical environment. It is an altar. It is a threshold space where the work of healing can unfold without the interference of the ordinary world — where silence is honored, where the body can exhale, where ceremony is possible.

Topanga Canyon itself is a participant in this work. The land here carries its own medicine: the chaparral, the live oaks, the creek that runs below, the fog that moves in from the ocean. Sessions at The Haven are always held in relationship with the more-than-human world that surrounds and holds them.

Formation

Lineage & Training

Adi Marie's practice is rooted in formal training and lived initiation across multiple lineages. She does not present herself as a self-taught practitioner of traditions she encountered as an outsider — she is a lineage holder, accountable to the teachers and traditions who formed her.

Nationally Licensed Massage Therapist

Sixteen-plus years of active licensure. Trauma-informed somatic therapeutics, neuromuscular therapy, and spiritually-oriented bodywork.

Initiated Dagara Diviner

Formally initiated into the Dagara divination tradition, carrying the Crocodile Clan lineage through the Somé family of Burkina Faso. Trained under elders Ivan Chocron, Yvonne, and Chad Charles.

Curandera — Mesoamerican & Caribbean Lineages

Practices rooted in indigenous ancestral medicine ways, herbalism, ceremony, and sacred relationship with the living world.

Medicine Woman & Priestess

Ordained as a medicine woman and priestess, carrying teachings across multiple sacred traditions with accountability to their lineage holders.

Specialized Trainings

Generative trance hypnosis · Drama therapy · Expressive arts · Ethnopharmacology · Bioenergetics · Somatic therapy · Mythopoetic facilitation

Ancestral Heritage

Multi-generational descendant of Mesoamerican, Caribbean, West African, Celtic, and Roma lineages — wisdom currents that run through both her practice and her personhood.

A Note

On Sacred Reciprocity

This practice operates on a model of sacred reciprocity — the understanding that exchange between practitioner and receiver is not merely transactional, but relational. It is an acknowledgment that this work has value, that the practitioner's time and training and devotion deserve honor, and that access to healing should not be determined by financial position alone.

A sliding scale is offered across all services. There are three tiers: Sustaining, Supporting, and Supported. No one is turned away for financial reasons. If you are navigating genuine financial hardship, reach out before booking — the conversation itself is part of the care.

For those with financial abundance, choosing the Supporting tier is a direct act of solidarity — it is what makes the Supported tier possible for others. This is the circle completing itself.
