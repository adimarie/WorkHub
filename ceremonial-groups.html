Group Ceremony

Ceremonial & Ritual Groups

Some thresholds can only be crossed together.

In the animist traditions that inform this work, community is not a convenience — it is medicine. The individual cannot complete certain healings alone, not because they lack strength, but because some things were broken in community and can only be repaired there. Some initiations require witnesses. Some grief only moves when it is held by the grief of others. Some thresholds are simply too large for a single person to cross without a circle at their back.

Ceremonial and Ritual Groups gather for many purposes: new and full moon circles that tend the rhythmic intelligence of the body in sync with the lunar cycle; grief rituals in the tradition of Sobonfu and Malidoma Some, where loss is given permission to be fully itself; ancestral healing ceremonies that open the lines between the living and the dead; initiation rites that mark what must be marked — the end of an old self, the crossing of a threshold that cannot be uncrossed; plant medicine circles held in sacred container with full preparation, guidance, and integration; and seasonal earth ceremonies that return us to the intelligence of the living world at its turning points.

When people gather in genuine ceremony, something arrives that could not have arrived for any one of them alone. The field itself becomes the healer.

Many gatherings are held at The Haven — a land-based ceremonial space in Topanga Canyon where the canyon oak and the sage and the stone have long held council. The land itself participates. Other gatherings move outdoors into the hills and arroyos of the Santa Monica Mountains, where the seasonal ceremonies are held as close as possible to the earth that the season is turning in. The setting is never incidental. Where ceremony is held matters.

Custom group containers are available for private communities, organizations, therapeutic practices, and intentional gatherings of any kind. If you are holding a community that needs ceremony — a rite of passage, a grief ritual, an ancestral healing, an initiation — reach out. These containers are built together, shaped to the particular need of the group that is calling for them.

Upcoming public gatherings are announced through the newsletter and on Instagram. Follow along there to know when the next circle is forming.
