Platicas — Guidance & Counsel

Conversations held in ceremony, in the presence of the unseen.

In the curandera tradition, a platica is not ordinary conversation. The word itself — from the Spanish, meaning talk or chat — does not fully convey what happens when two people sit together in sacred intention, with ceremony opened, with the ancestors and helping spirits invited to be present. A platica is a conversation held in a larger field than the two people in the room. It is counsel that comes not only from the practitioner's knowledge, but from the wisdom that moves through when the conditions are right.

Adi Marie brings sixteen years of therapeutic practice — grounded in somatic approaches, trauma-informed care, and relational depth — alongside her training in animist healing and her long relationship with her own ancestral and elemental guides. What she offers in a platica is not therapy in the clinical sense, and it is not a reading. It is accompaniment: a rare quality of presence that holds the full complexity of who you are without reduction, and that listens at multiple levels simultaneously — to your words, to your body, to what the unseen world may be wanting you to know.

What you are seeking is not advice. It is accompaniment — someone who can sit with you inside the question, without needing to resolve it before its time.

Platicas are for those at a crossroads: when the path forward is genuinely unclear, when two real things are in genuine tension, and when a well-meaning friend or therapist cannot quite reach the register where the real question lives. They are for integration after ceremony or plant medicine, when what opened needs a witness who can understand its terrain. For those in the midst of major initiation — the death of a long identity, the end of a relationship, the emergence of a new calling. For grief that has nowhere else to land. For the complexity of relationship, of creative emergence, of spiritual calling, of the question that keeps returning no matter how many times you think you have answered it.

What a Session Involves

Each platica begins with a brief opening ceremony — a moment of intentional arrival that distinguishes what follows from ordinary conversation. From there, you speak and Adi Marie listens: not to diagnose or guide, but to hear you fully and to notice what arrives in that listening. She may reflect, ask, offer a perspective from her own training and ancestral lineage, or simply witness. Sessions close with a short grounding practice and any practical integration that the conversation calls for. Platicas are available in person at The Haven in Topanga Canyon or via video call.

Duration 60–90 minutes. Available as a single session or ongoing series.
Scale Offered on Sacred Reciprocity sliding scale. No one is turned away for financial reasons.
